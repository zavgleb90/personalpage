About

I am an Assistant Professor of Artificial Intelligence in the College of Business at Auburn University at Montgomery (AUM). My research lies at the intersection of applied artificial intelligence and machine learning, healthcare information technology, operations research, data science, and the science of science.

I study how information technologies and advanced analytical methods improve organizational and healthcare decision-making. I also examine how academic disciplines, journals, universities, and research institutions evolve over time.

My work has appeared in Production and Operations Management and Decision Support Systems. My current projects address healthcare information systems, AI governance, organizational creativity, freight transportation, dynamic decision-making, and the development and evaluation of academic fields and journals.

At AUM, I serve as Co-Director of the Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence program and Project Leader for the Alabama Talent Development Project.

Research

Across my research streams, my goal is to combine methodological innovation with practical impact—advancing knowledge while generating insights that inform business practice, healthcare decision-making, organizational performance, and the development of research institutions.

Research Overview

  • Healthcare IT and decision models: predictive and prescriptive analytics that support healthcare decisions, improve patient engagement, and address operational challenges such as missed appointments and clinical uncertainty.
  • Applied AI and machine learning: analytical and AI-enabled systems for organizational decision-making, freight transportation, and other applied business settings.
  • AI governance: national regulatory configurations and their relationships with AI innovation, investment, and ecosystem performance.
  • Knowledge management, IT capability, and creativity: how knowledge-management strategies, communication channels, and organizational IT capabilities jointly influence team creativity and innovative behavior.
  • Science of science and research evaluation: patterns in author, journal, disciplinary, and institutional performance across large-scale publication datasets, including FT50, UTD24, and Academic Journal Guide outlets.
  • Dynamic decision-making: Bayesian and real-options approaches for decisions made under uncertainty, including healthcare and product-goal expansion.

Teaching

My teaching interests include data analytics, machine learning and artificial intelligence for business, information technology and systems, information security, and business programming.

Teaching Philosophy

I aim to create a learner-centered environment that combines challenging content with applied activities. I regularly use semester-long projects, modular assignments, demonstrations, and realistic organizational problems to help students connect concepts with practice.

I also work intentionally to establish a safe learning environment where questions are welcomed, technical experimentation is encouraged, and mistakes are treated as opportunities for growth.
