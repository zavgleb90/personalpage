About

I am an Assistant Professor of Information Systems in the College of Business at Auburn University at Montgomery (AUM). My research lies at the intersection of data analytics, machine learning and artificial intelligence, operations research, and the science of science. I study how information technologies and advanced analytics improve decision-making in business and healthcare, and how research productivity and institutional performance evolve across disciplines and journals.

My work has appeared in Decision Support Systems and Production and Operations Management, and I have ongoing projects spanning healthcare IT, knowledge management and creativity, and bibliometric analyses of leading business and IS journals.

Research

Across my research streams, my goal is to combine methodological innovation with practical impact—advancing knowledge while generating insights that inform business practice, healthcare decision-making, and the development of research institutions.

Research Overview

  • Healthcare IT & decision models: predictive and prescriptive analytics to support high-stakes clinical decisions (e.g., Bayesian optimal stopping with dynamic patient trajectories).
  • Knowledge management, IT capability & creativity: how codification vs. personalization KM strategy, communication channels, and IT capability jointly shape team creativity (multilevel evidence).
  • Science of science & research productivity: patterns in author, journal, discipline, and institutional performance across large-scale datasets (FT50 / UTD24 and related bibliometrics).

Teaching

My teaching interests include Business Analytics (data analytics, statistical data mining), business programming (R/Python), and AI/ML for business applications (deep learning, NLP, and applied AI).

Teaching Philosophy

I aim to create a learner-centered environment that combines challenging content with applied activities. I regularly use semester-long projects broken into modules and in-class assignments that help students connect concepts to practice. I also work intentionally to establish a safe learning environment where questions are welcomed and mistakes are treated as opportunities for growth.

Service

I view service as a bridge connecting research, teaching, and societal impact. At AUM, I serve as Co-Director of the Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence (MSAI) program and Project Leader for the Alabama Talent Development Project, a multi-year state-supported initiative to expand AI capacity in Alabama. My service focuses on program development, interdisciplinary collaboration, and expanding equitable access to AI and analytics education.
